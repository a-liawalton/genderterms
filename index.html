What it means to be non-binary.

Developed from the term genderqueer, which was coined by Riki Anne Wilchins in the mid-1990s, non-binary means a spectrum of gender identities that are not exclusively masculine or feminine‍—‌identities that are outside the gender binary. (Other terms used are "enby" and "nonbinary" (without the hyphen).

Non-binary captures an umbrella of other terms. Some other terms people might use to describe their non-binary gender identity are included below. These terms and definitions are taken from the Wikipedia entry on Non-binary gender:

Agender
Those who identify as having no gender or being without a gender identity.
Bigender
A gender identity that includes any two gender identities and behaviors.
Typically understood to mean that one identifies as both male and female or moves between masculine gender expression and feminine gender expression, having two distinct gender identities simultaneously or fluctuating between them.
Demigender
A gender identity of a person identifying partially or mostly with one gender and at the same time with another gender.
Genderfluid
People who often express a desire to remain flexible about their gender identity rather than committing to a single definition.
Transfeminine
May be used by individuals who were assigned male at birth but align more closely with femininity, while not necessarily fully identifying as a woman.
Transmasculine
May be used by individuals who were assigned female at birth but align more closely with masculinity, while not necessarily fully identifying as a man.

Non-binary is a term that gaining more traction. People that identify as non-binary belong to the LGBTQ* group.
